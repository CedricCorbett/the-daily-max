What if the weakest person on your team could tie the strongest? 💪

That's the whole point of The Daily Max.

It's a free app I built. No ads. No paywall. No catch.

Here's the deal:

Every day you hit 4 stations — push-ups, squats, hollow hold, and pull-ups.

You don't score against me. You don't score against the beast down the street. You score against YOUR own personal best.

Hit 100% of your PR and you max out. That's it. The app caps you at 1.0 on purpose.

So a 55-year-old doing 10 push-ups can post the same score as a gym monster doing 100. Your only opponent is yesterday's you.

Why I built it this way 👇

Every fitness app I've tried rewards people who are already strong. Newbies get buried. Crews fall apart. The "fit one" drags everyone else along until the rest quit.

The Daily Max flips that.

A titan CANNOT carry a sleeping crew. Everyone has to show up. That's the rule the whole app is built on.

And this is where it gets fun.

You team up in CREWS and go head-to-head with other crews. 🔥

Each person logs their day. Your crew's score is the average. Skip a day? You drag your whole team down. Show up? You lift them.

No cash prizes. No leaderboard gimmicks. Just your name next to your people, and another crew across the line trying to out-commit you.

Who it's for:
— People who want to move every day
— People who quit apps because they felt behind
— People who want a squad, not a solo grind
— Busy parents, shift workers, beginners, vets… anyone with a body

The method is simple: strip out the fluff, cap the carry, reward the showing up. Commitment over volume. Discipline over peak.
